Deriving Variational Message Passing

Will Wolf

 November 23, 2018

Notes

  • The update for one of the independent variational factors is the same as per normal: it's the equation given by MFVB.
  • When you look at the terms involved, you realize that it's a sum, with contributions from the Markov blanket--the parents, children, and co-parents--of the node in question.
  • This is the case because it is only the terms that include \(H_j\) that belong in the variational likelihood of variable \(H_j\). This is a straightforward point.
  • An expectation is taken of this sum, over all variational factors except \(Q(H_j)\). Note that: \(H_j\), which need appear in a function that concerns \(H_j\), is utterly distinct from \(Q(H_j)\) (the function is given by an expectation over all variational factors except \(Q(H_j)\)).
  • By using conjugate-exponential variational factors, the sum that gives the optimal, updated \(Q^{*}(H_j)\) (a posterior) gives a distribution with same form as the pre-update \(Q^(H_j)\) (the prior).
  • As such, we can say, tautologically, that the update only changes the value's of the distribution's parameters.
  • Conjugate-exponential distributions implies: the child distribution's functional form can be rewritten such that the natural statistic vector is that of its parent (for all parents).
  • However, the authors argue that is ultimately incidental; in rewriting \(P(\text{child}|Y)\) for a given parent \(Y\), isolate \(Y\) in the natural statistic vector which, because of the pre-ordained conjugacy, will deterministically give you the \(\mathbf{u}_{\mathbf{Y}}(Y)\) of \(P(Y)\).
  • In turn, this gives you \(\phi_{XY}\), without having had to make reference to \(P(Y)\) and its form.
  • The reparameterizations of the child distribution w.r.t. the natural statistic of each of its parents are all equal, of course.
  • As such, the child distribution is a multi-linear function of the respective natural statistics.
  • A multi-linear function of variables a, b, c is one linear w.r.t. a, b and c. For example, y = 2a + 3b + 5c.
  • If there are two reparameterizations, we might rewrite the child distribution as: child_distribution = 2 * reparam_parent_1 - reparam_parent_2, which would be multi-linear in the natural statistics of the parent distributions.
  • Now, turn back to the optimal MFVB update at the beginning:
  • As this is a function of \(Y\), it only involves distributions over \(Y\).
  • All of these distributions--which form the Markov blanket of \(Y\)--can be rewritten in terms of the natural statistic of \(Y\).
  • (Similarly, within the MB, we could reparameterize one w.r.t. the natural statistic of another, then "propagate" this reparameterization to the rest, i.e. write the rest in terms of the same natural statistic., as all adjacent nodes in the MB are conjugate-exponential by definition.)
