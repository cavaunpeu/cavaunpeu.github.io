notes

the partition function

  • normalizes an unnormalized probability distribution
  • obtained by summing over all the variables in that distribution
  • a scalar -- literally just a sum of all of the values in the \(\phi\) column of a given factor

why it might be hard to compute

  • when we have a lot of rows in the factor
  • we would have a lot of rows because our factor's variables take on many different values

the evidence term

  • has nothing to do with the partition function
  • marginalize out/sum over something

why it might be hard to compute

  • the thing over which we are marginalizing takes on a lot of different values
  • for example, a softmax where \(y\) takes on 1 billion different values
  • necessarily, we'd have to compute 1 billion rows of the joint distribution
  • thereafter, we sum, but this is the easy part.
